# The Three Body Problem

The Three Body Problem is one of classical physics' most thought about questions, beginning with Newton who eventually left it unsolved. In general, the n-body problem 
asks whether the equations in a gravitational system of n physical bodies with similar mass can be completely solved. With typical Newtonian mechanics, the one and two
body problems were able to completely be solved by Newton, but later Poincare showed that a closed form solution was impossible to generate. To be clear, n-body systems
are fully deterministic, yet there are simply too many unknown variables to have a solvable system.

However, certain simplifications lead to solvable systems; for example, the reduced three body problem with two large masses and one smaller one was shown to be solved 
using Poincare's Perturbation Theory, and though we cannot generate full solutions, the method of numerical integration where the conditions of each individual timestep
are used to approximate the next, instead of attempting to forecast solutions further in advance.
