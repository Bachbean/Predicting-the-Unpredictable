## Summer Research Project: `Predicting-the-Unpredictable`
*Bach Kieu, Bloomington High School South, Indiana*

### Objective:
In classical physics, many physical systems can be described by deterministic laws, meaning that their future behavior is governed by precise mathematical rules. For example, the motion of planets, projectiles, and other objects can often be predicted accurately using the laws of classical mechanics. Yet some deterministic systems exhibit much more complex behaviors, where tiny differences in initial conditions can lead to dramatically different outcomes over time. This phenomenon, which is known as chaos, places fundamental limits on our ability to predict the future.

This summer research project introduces a fascinating field of nonlinear dynamical systems and chaos theory. The goal is to explore how complex and seemingly random behavior can emerge from simple mathematical equations, and why chaotic dynamics are found throughout nature at the advanced high-school level. Through a combination of literature review, mathematical concepts, computer simulations, and real-world examples, the nature of chaos and its implications for scientific prediction will be learned.
